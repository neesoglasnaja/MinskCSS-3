Time is money

Why again

  • To sell your time to a client
  • The list of tasks should be known
  • To plan your or other's work

Types of estimation

  • Preliminary estimation — high-level estimation
  • Accurate estimation — low-level estimation
  • Revaluation of timings — something has changed (specifications, team, etc.)

Common mistakes

  • People do mistakes
  • Lack of information or insufficient list of tasks
  • Risks are not included
  • It's hard to estimate new things: technologies, third-party API, etc.
  • Every developer is good (and bad) at something
  • Low personal responsibility

How to learn

Step 0. Track your time

  • The 20 Best Time-Tracking Apps (article)

1. Make everything clear

  • Ask questions
  • Find all bottlenecks
  • Give best and worst estimations instead of an exact number

2. Be more detailed

  • Decompose high-level tasks
  • Think of the (exact) implementation
  • Do not decompose everything

3. Think together

  • Assign time estimations to a few developers
  • Do time estimation reviews

4. Make risks visible

  • Disclose a particularly unpredictible tasks
  • Every member of the team should be aware of all risks
  • Do research before implementing 'unknown' tasks
  • Don't forget about timing revaluation

5. Do not forget about youself

  • Remember, you don't spend 100% of your working hours on coding
    • Reading docs, API refs, etc.
    • Deploy, git, code reviews
    • Testing
    • Meetings
    • Learning
    • Coffee/cookies, emails, small talks, procrastination

6. Re-think your timings

  • Confirm an estimation before you start to work on the features
  • Discuss excact implementation with the team

7. Scream when it's crucial

  • Don't be afraid of asking for help
  • Inform your team/PM/client about time issues as soon as you get the idea you'll have them
  • Team/PM/client are humans and you could convince them to make a different decision/timings/etc.

8. Analyze your work

  • Time estimation is not about predictions
  • Gather your experiense and analyze estimated and logged time
  • Do "retro" on over- and underestimated tasks
  • Calculate your index of estimation errors

Bonus. PM's tips

  • Improve communicative skills (yours or/and your team)
  • Motivate your team to learn how to estimate
  • Try to group people in the office by some attribute (departments/projects)
  • Implement different development methodologies (waterfall, agile, extreme programming)
